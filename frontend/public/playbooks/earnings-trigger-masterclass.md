**Chapter 1 • Why Earnings Are The Truth-Test**
===============================================

Earnings are the single moment in the year when the management\'s
narrative collides with reality. Everything else --- interviews,
conference appearances, brokerage notes, Twitter threads, channel-check
whispers --- is opinion. The income statement is fact. The cash flow
statement is more fact. The order book is the strongest fact. The
earnings cycle is the truth-test of every investment thesis.

**There are only three ways an Indian equity returns capital to its
investor: (1) capital appreciation driven by earnings growth, (2)
capital appreciation driven by multiple expansion,** (3) dividends and
buybacks. The first two combine multiplicatively. A company that grows
EPS 30% with PE rising from 20x to 30x delivers 95% total return. A
company that grows EPS 30% with PE falling from 20x to 15x delivers -3%.
Same earnings, opposite outcomes. The multiple is doing 60% of the work.

This is why two stocks with identical EPS growth can perform
diametrically opposite. Polycab and HUL both grew earnings \~15-20%
across FY22-FY26. Polycab\'s multiple expanded from 25x to 50x. HUL\'s
compressed from 62x to 55x. Polycab tripled. HUL was flat. The
difference is not earnings. The difference is the market\'s perception
of forward earnings --- that is what the PE multiple captures, and that
perception is updated quarter by quarter.

**THE FUNDAMENTAL EQUATION** Stock Return = (1 + EPS Growth) x (1 + PE
Re-rating) + Dividend Yield - 1. This is the only equation that matters.
If you cannot articulate both inputs ahead of the print, you are
speculating, not investing.

The earnings calendar in India runs four times a year --- April-May for
Q4, July-August for Q1, October-November for Q2, January-February for
Q3. Within each window, 5,000+ listed companies report. The
institutional investor sees ALL of them but trades only 20-40 per
quarter where conviction is high and the math works. The retail investor
sees a fraction and reacts to headlines. The gap between these two
outcomes --- institutional and retail post-earnings returns --- is
precisely captured by how seriously each respects the seven variables in
the framework above. This document gives the retail investor the
institutional discipline. The next 100 pages are the playbook.

**2.1 William O\'Neil --- CANSLIM\'s \'C\' Is The Most Important Letter**
-------------------------------------------------------------------------

*\'The percentage increase in earnings per share is by far the single
most important element in stock selection today. The greater the
percentage increase, the better.\'*

William O\'Neil founded Investor\'s Business Daily after his
quantitative research at Hayden Stone in the late 1950s. He back-tested
every multibagger from 1953 onwards and found a consistent pattern:
before the stock broke out, EPS in the most recent quarter had grown at
least 25% YoY. The median CANSLIM winner showed EPS growth of 70% in the
quarter preceding the breakout, with sequential acceleration --- Q-1
+25%, Q +40%, Q+1 +60%.

O\'Neil\'s operational rule: Reject any stock where EPS growth in the
most recent quarter is below 25% YoY. Prefer 40%+. Require accompanying
revenue growth of 25%+ so the earnings cannot be cost-cut manufactured.
Combine with a sound base structure (cup-handle or VCP) and an RS rating
of 80 or higher. This is the C-A-N-S-L-I-M filter: Current Quarterly
Earnings, Annual Earnings, New (product/management/high), Supply/Demand
(float and volume), Leader/Laggard, Institutional Sponsorship, Market
Direction. The C is alphabetically first by design.

**O\'NEIL ACTION RULE** If Q EPS growth less than 25% YoY, you are not
in a CANSLIM stock --- you are in a hold or a sell. Period. No
exceptions, no excuses about lumpy quarters or one-time items.
Twenty-five-percent is the floor.

**2.2 Peter Lynch --- The Stock Follows The Earnings**
------------------------------------------------------

*\'The stock follows the earnings. If a company is growing earnings at
20% a year and another at 10%, over a decade the difference in stock
price will be enormous, regardless of starting valuation.\'*

Peter Lynch managed Magellan Fund for 13 years (1977-1990) compounding
at 29.2% annually. His framework, codified in One Up On Wall Street, is
the PEG ratio: Price-to-Earnings divided by Earnings Growth rate. A PEG
below 1.0 is cheap; 1.0-1.5 is fair; above 2.0 is expensive. A 25 PE
stock growing earnings at 25% has a PEG of 1.0. A 25 PE stock growing at
10% has a PEG of 2.5 --- expensive, regardless of how strong the
franchise feels.

Lynch\'s second principle: five-year sustainable EPS growth, not
one-quarter pops. He distinguished between cyclicals (where Q-by-Q
matters for entry timing), tenbaggers (where multi-year hold matters),
stalwarts (where steady 10-12% growth justifies a 15-18x multiple), slow
growers (where dividend yield is the entire thesis), and turnarounds
(where the trough quarter matters less than the inflection signal).

**LYNCH ACTION RULE** Compute PEG for every position quarterly. Trim
positions where PEG climbs above 2.0. Add positions where PEG falls
below 1.0 due to either price decline or earnings acceleration. Track
the change in PEG quarter-by-quarter as your earliest signal that the
thesis is improving or deteriorating.

**2.3 Stanley Druckenmiller --- Go For The Jugular**
----------------------------------------------------

*\'When you have tremendous conviction on a trade, you have to go for
the jugular. It takes courage to be a pig.\'*

Druckenmiller, Soros\'s lieutenant during the 1992 Pound break and later
founder of Duquesne (29.4% annualized for 30 years), positions ahead of
earnings inflections --- never after them. His framework: identify macro
and sectoral inflections 6-12 months before consensus catches up. Read
commodity prices, FX, rates, central bank signals as leading indicators
of corporate earnings. When conviction is high, size aggressively. He
sized at 6-7% of capital regularly; in extraordinary conviction setups
he went to 10%+.

On earnings: \'The worst trade is to stay neutral when conviction is
high --- that is the asymmetry you are paid to capture.\' Translation:
if you have done the work to anticipate an earnings inflection (e.g.,
HAL\'s Tejas order book accelerating, Solar Industries\' defence export
ramp post-Pulwama, Praj\'s biofuel order intake post-E20 mandate), the
position needs to be meaningful --- not 1% --- or the work is wasted.

**DRUCKENMILLER ACTION RULE** When all six variables align
(acceleration, margin direction, multiple discount, guidance language,
chart stage, sector flow), size to 4-6% on a single name. Pyramid to
7-8% only on confirmed Stage-2 breakout post-earnings. A 1.5% position
on a 5-bagger is worth less than half a 4% position on a 3-bagger.

**2.4 Warren Buffett --- Earnings Can Be Pliable As Putty**
-----------------------------------------------------------

*\'Earnings can be as pliable as putty when a charlatan heads the
company reporting them. Cash flow is fact; profit is opinion.\'*

Buffett\'s filter is owner earnings = Net Income +
Depreciation/Amortization - Maintenance Capex - Working Capital changes.
A consistent gap between reported PAT and operating cash flow over three
or more years is a red flag. India has produced multiple cases ---
Sintex Industries (PAT positive for years while cash flow negative;
eventually NCLT), Jain Irrigation (PAT vs OCF divergence; ICRA downgrade
BB-Negative FY26), Future Group (chronic OCF deficit until insolvency),
McLeod Russel (tea estates), Suzlon pre-2020 (debt restructuring). The
pattern is consistent: receivables-bloated infrastructure / EPC /
capex-heavy businesses where revenue can be \'bought\' by extending
credit terms.

Buffett\'s quality filter: ROE \>15%, low debt (D/E \<0.5), predictable
earnings (sigma of EPS growth less than 30% of the mean), durable
competitive advantage. If a business does not meet these criteria,
single-quarter earnings beats are not a thesis --- they are noise.

**BUFFETT ACTION RULE** For every Indian position, calculate trailing
3-year OCF / PAT. If less than 80%, the earnings quality is suspect. If
less than 60%, it is a sell regardless of the headline beat. The cash
flow statement is the truth.

**2.5 Howard Marks --- The Market Rewards Surprise**
----------------------------------------------------

*\'The market doesn\'t reward what\'s known; it rewards what surprises.
The biggest re-rating happens when the variant view becomes
consensus.\'*

Howard Marks runs Oaktree Capital and writes the most-read memo in
institutional investing. His framework --- second-level thinking ---
refuses to ask \'is this a good company?\' That is first-level.
Second-level: \'is the market underestimating how good?\' The biggest
stock returns come when the variant perception becomes consensus, i.e.,
the moment a non-consensus thesis crosses the threshold into the
mainstream brokerage notes.

Earnings season is precisely the test of variant perception. If you
believed in Aditya Vision\'s Tier-2/3 dominance in 2021 when no broker
covered the stock, the moment HDFC Securities initiated coverage in 2023
was the variant view becoming consensus --- that is the re-rating from
12x to 70x PE. If you believed in MTAR\'s clean energy ramp in March
2021 at IPO, the moment Bloom Energy\'s revenue contribution became
visible to street consensus in Q3 FY22 was the variant view
crystallizing. The stock had already 2x\'d before consensus \'caught
up\'; that consensus crystallization was what drove the next 2x.

**MARKS ACTION RULE** For every position, ask: what is the variant view
I am holding versus consensus? If you cannot articulate the variant view
in one sentence, you are following consensus and the outsized return is
not available to you. Variant views must be defended with primary
research, not headlines.

**2.6 Mark Minervini --- Stage 2 Plus Earnings Acceleration**
-------------------------------------------------------------

*\'The fastest way to make money in stocks is to buy companies whose
earnings are growing fastest, in industries whose growth is fastest, at
the moment their stocks break out of a sound base.\'*

Mark Minervini won the 1997 US Investing Championship with 155% return;
his SEPA (Specific Entry Point Analysis) framework requires four
simultaneous conditions: (1) Stage 2 uptrend (price above 30-week SMA,
both rising), (2) 3 consecutive quarters of EPS acceleration, (3) sales
acceleration matching EPS, (4) Relative Strength rating 80+ versus the
broad market. Add a VCP (Volatility Contraction Pattern) base of 12-18
weeks with progressively shallower pullbacks on declining volume, then
breakout above the pivot on volume 2x+ average.

Indian application: Polycab\'s chart from 2021 through 2026 is a
textbook Stage 2 with multiple VCP bases at higher levels --- each base
resolving on earnings prints. Aditya Vision 2020-2025 same pattern.
Shaily Engineering 2023-2024 same. The chart and the earnings work
together. Either alone is insufficient --- a beat without a base reaches
no further; a base without a beat fails the breakout.

**MINERVINI ACTION RULE** Do not buy stocks in Stage 1 (basing without
breakout) or Stage 4 (downtrend). Only Stage 2 (uptrend with earnings
acceleration) or early Stage 3 (early distribution but momentum intact).
Stage 1 = research; Stage 2 = buy; Stage 3 = trail stops; Stage 4 = exit
and short.

**2.7 Ashish Kacholia --- Quarterly Earnings Are The Truth-Test**
-----------------------------------------------------------------

*\'One bad quarter is data, two bad quarters is trend, three bad
quarters means the story is broken.\'*

Ashish Kacholia, founder of Lucky Investments, built one of India\'s
most-watched portfolios --- Beta Drugs, Shaily Engineering, Safari
Industries, Aditya Vision, NIIT Learning, Mold-Tek Packaging, Vidhi
Specialty, Aditya Vision again at higher levels. His public portfolio
adds 25-30% CAGR over decade-plus. His discipline: buy emerging mid-caps
at sub-Rs 2,000 Cr mcap where each quarter must validate the multi-year
thesis. The exit signal is two consecutive disappointing quarters; the
position survives short-term volatility but not thesis-break.

Kacholia\'s 12-filter framework --- revenue growth 15%+, EBITDA growth
15%+, ROCE 15-20%, D/E less than 0.5, promoter holding above 50%, pledge
under 10%, industry duopoly preferred, exports 20%+, pricing power, mcap
Rs 500-8,000 Cr sweet spot, capacity expansion visible, free float
adequate --- is built around the quarterly print being the validation
moment. Each earnings cycle re-scores the 12 filters. A name that scored
11/12 last quarter falling to 8/12 this quarter triggers a portfolio
review.

**KACHOLIA ACTION RULE** Set 12-filter scores for every position at
quarter-end. Trim positions where score falls 2+ points. Exit fully if
score falls 3+ points. Do not add to positions where score is rising on
revenue alone --- wait for EBITDA growth to confirm. The 12-filter
discipline is what separates compounders from cyclical traps.

**2.8 Marcellus --- Quality Earnings Compounded Over Decades**
--------------------------------------------------------------

*\'We don\'t trade earnings; we own the franchise. Pricing power means
earnings are mechanically reliable.\'*

Saurabh Mukherjea and the Marcellus team built the Consistent
Compounders Portfolio (CCP) thesis: companies with ROCE above 15% and
revenue growth above 10% for 10 consecutive years create multi-decade
compounding. Marcellus favors Asian Paints, Pidilite, Page Industries,
Nestle India, Bajaj Finance, HDFC Bank, ICICI Bank, Titan, Avenue
Supermarts, Marico. The quality premium (40-60x PE) is justified by the
low standard deviation of earnings --- these companies have not had a
single year of EPS decline since 2005, in some cases since 1995. That
predictability is what justifies the multiple.

The Marcellus framework is the opposite of Druckenmiller\'s.
Druckenmiller buys macro inflection. Marcellus buys franchise.
Druckenmiller holds 18 months. Marcellus holds 18 years. Both work; both
make money; both are disciplined. The lesson: there is more than one
valid earnings-based framework. Pick yours and stick to it.

**MARCELLUS ACTION RULE** For quality compounders, the question is not
\'is this quarter strong\' but \'has the franchise been damaged\'. ROCE
deterioration beyond 200 bps over 4 quarters, market share loss over 200
bps to a credible competitor, governance event, or capital allocation
misstep are the sell triggers. Single-quarter earnings noise is not.

**2.9 Vijay Kedia --- SMILE Framework Plus Inflection Quarter**
---------------------------------------------------------------

*\'SMILE --- Small in size, Medium in experience, Large in aspiration,
Extra-large in market potential.\'*

Vijay Kedia runs Kedia Securities; his publicly-disclosed positions
include Atul Auto (Tier-2/3 3-wheeler at the inflection of CNG
conversion), Cera Sanitaryware (premium sanitaryware in housing capex
up-cycle), Tejas Networks (5G optical), Sudarshan Chemical (specialty
pigment), Patel Engineering, Vaibhav Global. Kedia hunts the third or
fourth quarter after a strategic pivot --- when topline and margin both
inflect simultaneously. Quarter 1 post-pivot is too early (one print is
noise); Quarter 5 post-pivot is too late (consensus already there).

His operational rule: study the conference call transcript of the pivot
quarter (where management announces the new strategy), then wait. Track
the next 3 quarters for evidence the pivot is delivering. Buy the 4th
quarter when the EPS inflection is visible but the stock has not yet
re-rated. This is the highest-conviction entry zone.

**KEDIA ACTION RULE** Build a watchlist of companies that have made
strategic pivots in the last 18 months. Track each through quarterly
cycles. The 4th quarter post-pivot is the entry. The 8th quarter
post-pivot is the trim. The 12th quarter post-pivot is the exit.
Multi-bagger holding window: 24-36 months from entry.

**2.10 Mohnish Pabrai / Guy Spier --- Heads I Win, Tails I Don\'t Lose Much**
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

*\'The stock market is a device for transferring money from the
impatient to the patient.\' (Buffett, via Pabrai/Spier)*

Mohnish Pabrai\'s Dhandho framework (from the Patel-immigrant
motel-buying methodology): post-earnings drawdowns in QUALITY businesses
are buying opportunities. The market over-reacts to single-quarter
disappointment in compounders; the discount window typically lasts 1-3
months. Pabrai\'s Fiat (2012, post-recall fears), Tata Motors (2018,
post-JLR China weakness), Sun Pharma (2017, post-Halol issues) --- all
bought at multi-year lows during earnings-disappointment windows.

Guy Spier emphasizes the 30-day cooling-off rule --- no buying within 30
days of seeing the idea, which kills earnings-day FOMO. Spier learned
from Buffett: the discipline of WAITING for the market to reprice your
idea is what creates the margin of safety. Buying on earnings day is
fine only if the position has been pre-committed and the entry price was
pre-set.

**PABRAI/SPIER ACTION RULE** Never buy a new position on earnings day.
Either you pre-committed (entry zone set ahead) and execute
mechanically, or you wait 30 days for the dust to settle and reassess.
The 30-day rule eliminates 90% of bad earnings-day decisions.

**2.11 Charlie Munger --- Invert, Always Invert**
-------------------------------------------------

*\'It is remarkable how much long-term advantage people like us have
gotten by trying to be consistently not stupid, instead of trying to be
very intelligent.\'*

Munger\'s framework: invert the question. Instead of asking \'which
stocks will beat this quarter?\', ask \'which stocks would I never own
regardless of beats?\'. The exclusion list is more important than the
buy list. Munger excluded businesses with: (1) chronic governance
issues, (2) high debt cycles, (3) commodity pricing (no pricing power),
(4) capital-intensive with low ROIC, (5) terminal product risk (e.g.,
Kodak film, BlackBerry handsets). For Indian application: exclude
pledged-promoter names, restructured-debt names,
single-customer-concentration names, RPT-heavy names, even if Q4 prints
look strong. The beat is a trap when the underlying business has
structural deficit.

**MUNGER ACTION RULE** Build an EXCLUSION list of 50+ Indian stocks you
will NEVER own regardless of quarterly results. Update annually. The
discipline of NOT owning bad businesses is what creates the room to own
great ones. Avoid the unrecoverable.

**2.12 Nick Sleep --- Scale Economies Shared**
----------------------------------------------

*\'The reason we own Costco and Amazon is the same: as the business
grows, the management gives the savings back to the customer, creating a
flywheel.\'*

Nick Sleep\'s Nomad Investment Partnership returned 21% net for 13 years
(2001-2014) by concentrating on three Scale Economies Shared (SES)
businesses --- Costco, Amazon, Berkshire. The SES model: as the company
grows, unit costs decline; the management chooses to share those
declines with customers (lower prices, better service) rather than
expand margins; this creates a flywheel where the lower prices drive
higher volumes which drive lower costs again. The earnings appear LOWER
than peers (because gross margin is shared), but the moat compounds.

Indian application: Avenue Supermarts (DMart) has SES economics ---
every productivity gain goes into lower shelf prices, not margin
expansion. Page Industries reinvests scale gains into inventory width
and dealer margin. Asian Paints traditionally shared scale gains via
tinting machine subsidization. The Q-by-Q earnings of SES businesses
often look UNDERWHELMING (single-digit EBITDA margin growth) but the
franchise depth widens. The investor\'s task is to look past the
quarterly number and read the strategic discipline.

**SLEEP ACTION RULE** For SES businesses, judge management on
REINVESTMENT decisions, not Q-by-Q margin. A management that protects
margin during a competitive squeeze is harvesting; a management that
absorbs margin compression to widen the moat is investing in the
flywheel. The latter is the compounder.

**Chapter 3 • The PE x EPS Math --- What Actually Moves Stocks**
================================================================

The total return on an Indian equity over any holding period decomposes
mathematically into three components. Internalize this and you will
never again confuse a beat with a buy signal.

**THE MASTER EQUATION** Total Return = (1 + EPS Growth) x (1 + PE
Re-rating) + Dividend Yield - 1. Example: EPS grows 30% and PE expands
from 25x to 35x (+40% multiple), the math gives (1.30 x 1.40) - 1 = 82%
capital appreciation, plus \~1% dividend yield = \~83% total return.

**3.1 Decomposing The Multibagger Equation**
--------------------------------------------

Take MTAR Technologies as a worked example. From IPO at Rs 575 (March
2021) to peak at Rs 2,800 (November 2021), the stock delivered 387%
return in 8 months. The math:

-   EPS FY21 = Rs 11.2 per share. Forward EPS FY23E (run-rate from Q3
    FY22 beat) = Rs 22.4 per share. EPS growth: 2x = +100%

-   Entry PE: Rs 575 / Rs 11.2 = 51x. Peak PE: Rs 2,800 / Rs 22.4 =
    125x. PE re-rating: 51x to 125x = +145%

-   Combined: (1.0 + 1.0) x (1.0 + 1.45) - 1 = 2.0 x 2.45 - 1 = 4.9x - 1
    = +390%

-   Of which: 100/390 = 26% came from EPS, 74% came from multiple
    expansion

The MTAR move was THREE-QUARTERS multiple expansion. The earnings
doubled --- necessary but not sufficient. What converted the EPS
doubling into a 4-bagger was the market\'s willingness to pay 125x
instead of 51x. Why? Because the variant view (clean energy + nuclear +
defence as a multi-decade theme) became consensus. Howard Marks\'s
framework, in numbers.

**3.2 The Same Math In Reverse --- Why HUL Goes Sideways**
----------------------------------------------------------

HUL FY22 to FY26. EPS grew at \~10% CAGR over four years --- from Rs 38
to Rs 56 per share. EPS growth +47%. Yet the stock went from Rs 2,400 to
Rs 2,650 --- only +10%. Where did the other +37% earnings growth
disappear?

-   Entry PE FY22: Rs 2,400 / Rs 38 = 63x

-   Exit PE FY26: Rs 2,650 / Rs 56 = 47x

-   PE re-rating: 63x to 47x = -25%

-   Combined: (1.0 + 0.47) x (1.0 - 0.25) - 1 = 1.47 x 0.75 - 1 = 1.10 -
    1 = +10% capital appreciation

-   Of which: 47/-25 dynamic = multiple compression of -25% offsets most
    of +47% EPS growth

HUL did everything right operationally. Every quarter was a beat or
in-line. ROE held 100%+ throughout. But the entry PE was too high for
the growth rate. The PEG ratio (Lynch framework) was 63/10 = 6.3 ---
meaning the price was 6.3x more expensive than the growth justified. At
PEG above 2.0, multiple compression is the default outcome over
multi-year horizons regardless of operational quality. This is not bad
management --- this is bad ENTRY VALUATION.

**3.3 The Five Multiple-Driven Scenarios You Will Face**
--------------------------------------------------------

Every Indian equity earnings outcome falls into one of five scenarios.
Recognize which you are in to know what to do.

### **Scenario A: Beat + Acceleration + Multiple at Discount = Multibagger Setup**

EPS growth accelerating from 25% to 40%; PE is below 5-year median;
sector flow positive. This is the highest-probability multibagger setup.
Action: BUY into position at full size, hold 18-36 months, trail Stage
2. Examples: MTAR Q3 FY22, Shaily Engineering FY24 Q1, KPI Green FY25
throughout, Suzlon Q4 FY25.

### **Scenario B: Beat + Steady Growth + Multiple at Premium = Hold, Don\'t Buy**

EPS growth steady at 15-20%; PE is at or above 5-year mean. The
franchise is intact but the multiple offers no margin of safety. Action:
HOLD existing positions; do NOT add. Examples: Nestle, Asian Paints
(pre-Birla Opus), Pidilite. The compounding works but you must already
own at lower entry.

### **Scenario C: Beat + Deceleration + Multiple at Premium = SELL**

EPS still growing but rate decelerating from 25% to 18% to 15%; PE still
at premium. The market will compress the multiple to match the new
growth rate. Action: TRIM 50% on the print; exit fully if next quarter
confirms deceleration. Examples: Bajaj Finance Q3 FY24 (record absolute
PAT but AUM growth moderation), Asian Paints FY26 (margin pressure),
Britannia FY24.

### **Scenario D: Miss + Multiple Compression + Stage 2 Intact = Pullback Buy**

Quarter missed expectations; stock fell 8-15%; multi-year trend up;
30-week SMA support holding. The compounder is being temporarily
mispriced by an over-reactive market. Action: ADD to existing position
at the pullback. Examples: Polycab Q2 FY24 (RM cost spike, stock -8%
then recovered), Cyient FY25 Q4 (macro slowdown, stock -45% then
bottomed).

### **Scenario E: Miss + Guidance Cut + Stage 4 Breakdown = Capitulation Sell**

Multi-quarter underperformance + management lowering guidance + chart
breaks down through 30-week SMA. The thesis is broken. Action: SELL
entire position regardless of loss. Examples: Zee Learn 2021-2023, Adani
Wilmar post-Hindenburg, RattanIndia Power, Suzlon pre-2022. The pain of
taking a loss is less than the pain of compounding it.

**3.4 PEG In Practice --- The Lynch Calculator**
------------------------------------------------

Peter Lynch\'s PEG ratio is your single most important valuation tool
for earnings-driven positioning. Formula: PEG = PE / EPS growth rate.

-   PEG less than 1.0: Cheap. Entry zone for new positions. Examples
    June 2026: Chaman Lal Setia 1,208 Cr mcap PE 17x EPS growth 30% =
    PEG 0.57

-   PEG 1.0 to 1.5: Fair. Add only on technical breakout. Example:
    V-Mart Retail 6,290 Cr PE 30x EPS growth 24% = PEG 1.25

-   PEG 1.5 to 2.0: Full. Hold existing; do not add. Example: Interarch
    Building Products 3,650 Cr PE 28x EPS growth 31% = PEG 0.90 (cheap!)

-   PEG above 2.0: Expensive. Trim positions. Example: Asian Paints FY24
    PE 55x EPS growth 8% = PEG 6.9 (extreme overvaluation)

-   PEG above 3.0: Distress sell. Multiple compression inevitable.
    Example: HUL FY22 PE 63x EPS growth 10% = PEG 6.3 (which gave us the
    flat 4-year stock)

**3.5 Quality Compounder Premium vs Cyclical Discount**
-------------------------------------------------------

Not all PE multiples are equal. The market correctly assigns higher
multiples to higher-quality earnings. Five categories:

-   Quality compounder (Asian Paints, Pidilite, Page, Nestle, Titan,
    HDFC Bank historically): 40-60x PE justified at 15-20% EPS CAGR with
    low sigma (less than 10%)

-   High-quality emerging compounder (eMudhra, Rainbow Children\'s,
    Interarch, Apar Industries): 30-45x PE at 25-35% EPS CAGR

-   Cyclical (cement, metals, autos, banks): 8-15x trailing PE at peak

-   Story / hype / pre-revenue: avoid. No valuation discipline

magnitude. A 25x PE on Asian Paints is a hold; a 25x PE on Ambuja
Cements is a sell (above peak cycle multiple).

**Chapter 5 • Indian Losers Despite Earnings Beats --- Twenty Case Studies**

**5.1 HUL --- The Chronic Single-Digit Trap**
---------------------------------------------

**Period: FY24-FY25 • EPS growth \~7-10% • Stock essentially flat**

Across Q1 FY24 through Q2 FY25, HUL repeatedly delivered 2-4% volume
growth and 6-8% revenue growth --- marginal beats on PAT (Rs 2,500-2,700
Cr vs Rs 2,450 Cr consensus). Day-1 reaction: flat to -1%. Month-1
reaction: typically -3% to -5%. Pre-Q PE was 62x; post-Q PE compressed
to 55x over FY24. The math: EPS growth +7% offset by multiple
compression of 11% = net -4% absolute capital appreciation.

Why? Street wants 8%+ volume growth from HUL as a quality compounder;
anything below that triggers a \'structural slowdown\' narrative ---
rural weakness, premiumization not enough, market share concerns about
competitor activity. A beat at low growth is treated as a CEILING
CONFIRMATION, not a positive surprise. The market is signaling that the
next 12 months may also be 7% growth, which does not justify 62x PE.

**LESSON** In mature FMCG, a beat at low growth is treated as a ceiling
confirmation. The acceleration matters more than the level. HUL Q-beats
stopped working because each beat confirmed the same 7-8% growth band
--- the multiple compressed to match. Compounders with stretched
multiples need ACCELERATING growth to justify holding.

**5.2 Asian Paints --- Market Share Panic Trumps Beats**
--------------------------------------------------------

Sector-structure shift is the deepest valuation killer. Asian Paints had
enjoyed a quasi-monopoly multiple (45-60x PE) for two decades. The
arrival of Birla Opus signaled the end of monopoly. No quarterly beat
can repair the multi-decade multiple thesis. The market re-rates
immediately in anticipation of multi-year share loss.

**LESSON** When sector structure shifts, NO single-quarter beat repairs
the multi-year multiple thesis. Identify the structural shift (new
competitor, new technology, regulatory change) and exit BEFORE the next
quarter\'s print. Asian Paints holders who exited at Birla Opus
announcement saved 30%+ versus those who waited for evidence of share
loss in the prints.

**5.3 Britannia --- Volume Growth Decides The Multiple**
--------------------------------------------------------

**Period: FY24 • PAT beats but volume stuck 2-4% • Multiple compressed
17%**

Multiple quarters in FY24 saw PAT beats (Rs 530-560 Cr vs Rs 510 Cr est)
but volume growth stuck at 2-4%. Day-1: flat. Month-1: -2% to -7%. Pre-Q
PE 58x to post-Q 48x. EPS growth \~9% offset by \~17% multiple
compression = net -8% absolute.

For staples (Britannia, HUL, Marico, Dabur), VOLUME is the leading
indicator. Margin-led beats --- when revenue grows via price increase
but volume is flat or down --- are treated as unsustainable. The math:
if price grew 6% and volume grew 2%, revenue grew 8% but next quarter
the high base means tougher. If price grew 6% but volume DECLINED 1%,
the cycle is breaking; even the price increase will not sustain.

**LESSON** For staples, VOLUME is the truth-test. Margin-led beats with
low volume = SELL. Track volume growth as the primary metric; gross
margin as secondary. Britannia consumers buying less at higher prices =
same total revenue but a thinning customer base. The multiple corrects.

below the cost of equity (15%) means the unit economics of the existing
stores are weakening --- the new store additions are masking the
deceleration in the mature store base.

**LESSON** At 90x+ PE, single-digit SSSG triggers structural multiple
compression regardless of headline beats. Track SSSG separately for
stores aged 0-2 years vs 2-5 years vs 5+ years. When 5+ year stores
decelerate, the unit economics are turning. The PE compresses violently.

**5.5 Page Industries --- Premium Punishment**
----------------------------------------------

**Period: Q2 FY23 • Single miss kills 25% • Recovery takes 6 quarters**

Q2 FY23 saw a marginal miss on margins despite revenue line beat ---
stock fell 25% in two sessions. Pre-Q PE 75x to post-Q 55x. Even in-line
prints on subsequent quarters produced no rally as inventory destocking
narrative persisted.

At 70x+ PE, the stock is priced for perfection. A single miss
recalibrates the entire growth and margin assumption. Page had been the
perfect quality compounder for a decade --- but at 75x PE entering a
margin reset, the asymmetry was massive: in-line = -5%; small miss =
-25%.

**LESSON** At 70x+ PE, the asymmetry is brutal: in-line gives flat, miss
gives -20%. Position-size quality compounders accordingly --- never
above 4-5% even in the highest conviction, because a single miss can
wipe out a year of compounding.

**5.6 Nestle India --- Multiple Cap Despite Margin Expansion**
--------------------------------------------------------------

**Period: FY24-25 • Consistent \~11% EPS growth • Net -2% over 4
quarters**

Across FY24-25, Nestle delivered consistent 8-10% revenue growth with
200-300 bps margin expansion --- meaningful PAT beats. Stock essentially
sideways (Rs 2,400-2,650 range adjusted). Pre-Q PE 75x to post-Q 65x.
EPS growth +11% absorbed by \~13% multiple compression = net -2%.

Beats that confirm a known thesis don\'t move stocks. Multiples need NEW
information. Nestle\'s strong pricing power, premiumization, and rural
recovery were all KNOWN. Each quarterly print was the expected outcome,
not a surprise. The variant view that could have re-rated Nestle ---
significant acquisition, expansion into a new category, geographic
expansion --- did not materialize.

**LESSON** Beats that CONFIRM consensus do not move stocks. Stocks move
on NEW information. Track what the variant view could be for each
position; if there is no plausible variant view, the multiple cannot
expand and the stock follows EPS growth (and at 75x PE, that means
single-digit returns).

**5.7 Dr Lal PathLabs --- Disruption Fears Cap Multiples**

3-year question. The only thing that breaks the multiple compression is

exclusive contracts. Until then, the multiple compresses irrespective of
operational delivery.

**5.8 HDFC Life --- VNB Margin Is The Real KPI**

Q2-Q3 FY24 saw APE growth beats (+15-18%) but VNB margin compression

**LESSON** In insurance, MIX matters more than growth. ULIP-led top-line

**5.9 ICICI Lombard --- Combined Ratio Improvement But Stock Flat**

Single-quarter operational beats don\'t re-rate when structural cost

structurally. ICICI Lombard\'s operational beats only matter if they
prove the structural trend is reversing. They did not. Multiple
compressed.

**5.10 Bajaj Finance --- Q3 FY24 The Classic Guidance Moderation Case**
-----------------------------------------------------------------------

**Period: Q3 FY24 • Record PAT Rs 3,639 Cr • Stock FELL 7% in single
session**

Record PAT of Rs 3,639 Cr (a beat) but stock fell 7% Day-1, -12% over

31-32% to 28-29%; RBI restrictions on eCOM/Insta EMI cards spooked the
market. EPS growth \~22% but multiple compressed \~19% = net +3% only
despite blockbuster absolute numbers.

This is the CANONICAL case study of the playbook. Bajaj Finance
delivered the largest absolute PAT in its history. By all conventional
metrics, the quarter was exceptional. But the GUIDANCE was the killer
--- moderation from 31-32% AUM growth to 28-29% is a 300 bps
deceleration. The market re-priced the multiple to match the new growth
rate. Plus the RBI regulatory action on credit cards added uncertainty.

**LESSON** GUIDANCE MODERATION KILLS MORE THAN GUIDANCE BEATS REWARD.
Asymmetric payoffs: markets punish \'we now expect 25% growth\' (vs 30%)
2-3x harder than they reward \'we now expect 32%\' (vs 30%). Loss
aversion is institutional. Watch the guidance language as carefully as
the headline number.

**Why The Twenty Losers Pattern**
=================================

Across the twenty cases, FIVE structural reasons explain why beats
failed to translate to stock returns:

-   REASON 1: Entry multiple too high relative to growth rate. PEG above
    3.0 (HUL, Nestle, Asian Paints pre-Birla Opus, Britannia, Marico)
    means multiple compression is the default mathematical outcome over
    multi-year horizons.

-   REASON 2: Growth deceleration. Bajaj Finance Q3 FY24 is the
    canonical case --- record absolute numbers but decelerating growth
    rate triggered multiple compression. Britannia volume deceleration,
    DMart SSSG deceleration, HDFC Life VNB margin deceleration --- all
    the same pattern.

-   REASON 3: Sector structural shift. Asian Paints (Birla Opus), Dr Lal
    (Tata 1mg disruption), Tata Consumer (acquired growth dilution) ---
    multi-quarter structural headwinds that no single Q-beat addresses.

-   REASON 4: Group-level / regulatory overhang. Adani Wilmar
    (Hindenburg), Kotak (RBI action + succession), LIC (government
    supply overhang), Honasa (post-IPO de-rate) --- exogenous factors
    override operational delivery.

-   REASON 5: Margin quality concerns. Margin-led beats with thin volume
    (Britannia, HUL, Marico), ULIP-led top-line in insurance (HDFC
    Life), or commodity pricing pass-through (Adani Wilmar) treated as
    unsustainable.

**The Master Rule From Both Chapters**
======================================

**THE INSTITUTIONAL EDGE** A beat alone is NOT a buy signal. A beat AT
THE RIGHT MULTIPLE WITH THE RIGHT VARIANT VIEW IN THE RIGHT CHART STAGE
WITH THE RIGHT GUIDANCE TRAJECTORY is the buy signal. The four-variable
check is what separates the 8 wins from the 18 winners against the 20
losers chapter. Apply the check before you act on any post-earnings
move.

**Chapter 6 • Pre-Earnings Whisper Methodology --- The Edge**
=============================================================

The single greatest source of edge in earnings-driven investing is NOT
what consensus models. It is what consensus has NOT yet modeled. The
pre-earnings whisper methodology gives you a 4-8 week informational edge
over the broker analysts, who themselves get the data 1-3 weeks ahead of
retail.

**6.1 Channel Checks --- The Most Underrated Source**
-----------------------------------------------------

Channel checks mean talking directly to the customers, distributors,
dealers, suppliers, and employees of the target company. Done correctly,
channel checks provide the FORWARD INDICATION of the next quarterly
print 4-8 weeks before the print. Done poorly, they introduce bias and
over-confidence.

### **How to run effective channel checks**

-   Identify the 5-10 most representative dealers/distributors. For
    Maruti, that means dealers in 10 cities across 4 zones. For Bajaj
    Finance, that means 8-10 large franchise partners across consumer
    durables financing. For Asian Paints, that means 5-8 paint shop
    dealers in 3 cities.

-   Call OR visit them. Email surveys have a 5% response rate; phone
    calls 30-40%; in-person visits 80%+. Honest information requires
    effort to extract.

-   Standard questions: (a) what is monthly volume trend last 3 months
    vs same period last year? (b) what is the inventory level at your
    end - normal/high/low? (c) what is the pricing trend - any
    discounting? (d) what is the consumer sentiment? (e) are there any
    new SKU launches you are tracking? (f) what is the credit cycle -
    has it tightened or loosened?

-   30+ calls across geographies gives statistically meaningful read.
    Less than 10 calls is anecdotal.

-   Track the same set every quarter. Trend matters more than absolute
    level.

**6.2 Industry Data Sources --- The Macro Backdrop**
----------------------------------------------------

Channel checks are micro. Industry data is macro. The two must align for
high-conviction pre-Q positioning.

### **Indian industry data sources to monitor monthly**

-   CMIE (Centre for Monitoring Indian Economy) --- production data,
    capacity utilization, capital expenditure, employment trends. The
    single best paid source for industrial activity.

-   SIAM / FADA --- monthly auto wholesales (SIAM) and retail (FADA).
    Wholesales lag manufacturer prints by 2 weeks; retails by 1 week.
    Track 12-month rolling vs YoY for trend.

-   AIOCD AWACS --- pharmaceutical secondary sales (Indian
    Pharmaceutical Market data). Monthly read. Used by pharma analysts
    as the gold standard for India branded growth.

-   PowerGrid Corporation data --- power demand, peak demand, deficit.
    Indicates economic activity. Strong correlation with industrial
    production.

-   RailRadar / IndianRailwayInfo --- freight volumes by commodity
    (coal, cement, steel, fertilizer, food grain). Leading indicator of
    B2B industrial activity.

-   GST collections (monthly) --- nominal GDP proxy. Track ex-petroleum
    / ex-state-share. Sustained 12%+ growth indicates broad-based
    consumption.

-   CMS Info Systems data --- cash-in-transit volumes. Proxy for
    consumption activity, especially in Tier-2/3 cities. Available via
    management commentary.

-   Bharat Bill Pay transaction volumes --- bill payment growth. Proxy
    for urban household activity.

-   UPI transaction data (NPCI monthly release) --- digital consumption
    activity. Year-over-year growth above 35% indicates strong consumer
    momentum.

-   E-way bill data (CGST) --- interstate commercial goods movement.
    Leading indicator of manufacturing activity 1-2 months ahead.

**6.3 Insider Activity --- The Strongest Pre-Q Whisper**
--------------------------------------------------------

Insider buying or selling in the 30 days BEFORE the quarterly silent
period (typically 21 days before the result date) is statistically the
strongest pre-Q indicator. Promoters know the print 6-8 weeks before
announcement.

-   BSE/NSE insider disclosures are real-time public information. Track
    via screener.in or Trendlyne insider trading dashboard.

-   PROMOTER BUYING in open market 30-60 days before Q-result is the
    STRONGEST PRE-Q WHISPER SIGNAL. Examples: Vijay Kedia\'s Atul Auto
    promoter buying preceded multiple beat cycles.

-   ESOP-funded selling is NEUTRAL --- usually pre-planned for tax/cash
    needs.

-   OPEN MARKET PROMOTER SELLING in the 30 days before result = RED
    FLAG. The promoter has knowledge of bad Q ahead.

-   Director / senior management selling carries less signal weight but
    still matters when concentrated (3+ insiders selling in same
    window).

-   Promoter pledge changes --- pledge release suggests confidence in
    upcoming earnings; pledge increase suggests cash strain or fear.

**6.4 Block & Bulk Deals --- Smart Money Footprint**
----------------------------------------------------

BSE/NSE publish bulk and block deal sheets daily. Institutional
investors (FII, MF, PE) often build or trim positions in the 2-3 weeks
before earnings. Track this footprint.

-   Block deal: single transaction \>0.5% of equity at a negotiated
    price. Visible 24 hours after.

-   Bulk deal: any client\'s net buy/sell exceeding 0.5% of equity in a
    session. Visible end of day.

-   FII block buys 2-3 weeks before earnings have predictive power in
    mid-caps. Sustained accumulation = upgrade pending.

-   Domestic MF buying via bulk deals = often a Q1 rotation signal;
    track which AMC is buying.

-   Insider sell into block deals (promoter selling to institutional
    buyer) = mixed signal. Need to understand the buyer.

**6.5 Peer Concall Read-Across**
--------------------------------

This is one of the most underutilized edge tools. If Asian Paints
concall on Day 1 commentary signals \'volume decline\' and \'destocking
by dealers\', then 7-14 days later Berger, Kansai, Akzo will likely echo
the same. The pattern is consistent within sector.

-   UltraTech sets cement realization commentary; Shree, Ambuja, ACC
    follow within 7-10 days. Track UltraTech first.

-   HDFC Bank NIM pressure read-across to ICICI / Axis / Kotak with
    one-quarter lag. Track HDFC first.

-   Maruti\'s monthly wholesale + retail commentary reads across to
    Hyundai (private), Mahindra, Tata Motors PV. Lag 7-15 days.

-   HUL\'s volume + premiumization commentary reads across to Nestle,
    Dabur, Marico, Britannia with 7-21 day lag.

-   Bharti Airtel\'s ARPU commentary reads across to Vodafone Idea,
    Reliance Jio (private). Track Bharti first.

**6.6 Raw Material + Commodity Shock**
--------------------------------------

Pre-Q margin pressure is often visible 4-6 weeks ahead from commodity
moves. Track these in real-time.

-   Specialty chem: benzene, toluene, xylene, propylene, butadiene LME
    spreads

-   Pharma: KSM (key starting material) China prices, paracetamol API,
    intermediates

-   FMCG: palm oil (CPO), crude derivatives (LDPE, HDPE), wheat, sugar

-   Cement: pet coke (\$/tonne), diesel (\$/L), fly ash availability

-   Steel: coking coal, iron ore

-   Power: thermal coal benchmark, natural gas (Henry Hub)

-   Paints: crude derivatives, titanium dioxide (TiO2)

-   Tyre: natural rubber, synthetic rubber, carbon black

-   Branded apparel: cotton (futures + spot), polyester (PTA + MEG)

**6.7 Sell-Side Estimate Revision Direction**
---------------------------------------------

Bloomberg consensus EPS data is the institutional standard. Track
revision direction T-30/T-60/T-90 days into the print.

-   Upward revisions accelerating into the print = positive setup
    (consensus is catching up to your variant view)

-   Downward revisions = negative setup (consensus is realizing trouble;
    the stock has likely already moved)

-   DIRECTION matters more than absolute level --- a 5% upward revision
    is meaningful even if the EPS estimate is still below your private
    estimate

-   Net upgrade-to-downgrade ratio in trailing 4 weeks (Bloomberg
    analyst recommendations dashboard): swing from -0.3 to +0.4 is
    meaningful

**6.8 FII / DII Positioning --- Pre-Q Setup**
---------------------------------------------

NSDL FII flow data (daily) + AMFI MF holdings (monthly) reveal pre-Q
positioning.

-   Rising MF holding in the quarter preceding the print + FII
    accumulation = post-Q follow-through buying support

-   Sector rotation flows --- when FII is buying capital goods broadly,
    individual capital goods earnings are amplified

-   AMFI sector positioning vs Nifty 500 weight --- overweights indicate
    building conviction; underweights indicate disbelief

-   Promoter + FII + DII combined holding above 75% of equity = thin
    float; small flows can drive disproportionate price moves

**Chapter 7 • Concall Reading Framework --- Twelve Signals**
============================================================

The earnings call is where management\'s narrative meets analyst
questioning. Read the transcript, not just the headlines. Twelve
specific things to listen for.

**Signal 1 • Opening Statement Tone**
-------------------------------------

The first 5 minutes of the call sets the entire tone. Compare verbatim
language across 4 quarters. \'Strong quarter\' becoming \'satisfactory
quarter\' is a yellow flag --- even when the numbers are identical, the
language signals management\'s INTERNAL view.

Example: Bajaj Finance Q3 FY24 opening shifted from \'we delivered a
strong quarter\' (Q2 FY24) to \'we delivered a strong quarter despite a
challenging macro and regulatory environment\' (Q3 FY24). The
\'despite\' was the signal. The market read the moderating language
correctly: stock fell 7%.

**Signal 2 • Tone Shift Cautious to Upbeat = BUY**
--------------------------------------------------

Watch for unprompted forward-confidence. \'We are well placed to\...\',
\'demand visibility extends through Q3\...\', \'we see strong
tailwinds\...\' --- these phrases appearing for the first time in 4
quarters signal an internal inflection. Track each company\'s standard
language and flag deviations.

Example: Apar Industries Q3 FY24 concall --- first use of phrase
\'unprecedented demand visibility through FY26\'. Three months later,
the stock had moved 30%.

**Signal 3 • Tone Shift Confident to Hedging = SELL**
-----------------------------------------------------

\'Subject to monsoon\', \'ex-one-time\', \'barring macro headwinds\',
\'we expect normalization in coming quarters\' appearing for the first
time = red flag. Management is preparing the analyst community for a
moderation.

Example: Asian Paints Q4 FY25 --- first use of \'competitive intensity
has stepped up\' (in response to Birla Opus). Multiple compressed 23%
over the next 12 months.

**Signal 4 • The Guidance Language Ladder**
-------------------------------------------

In ascending order of conviction:

-   \'We hope to\...\' --- lowest conviction; often used for stretch
    goals

-   \'We expect to\...\' --- moderate conviction; conditional on
    environment

-   \'We are targeting\...\' --- internally committed but not yet
    contractually visible

-   \'We are confident that\...\' --- high conviction; usually backed by
    order book

-   \'We are committed to\...\' --- public commitment; failure to
    deliver hurts credibility

-   \'We will deliver\...\' --- strongest; usually only for the
    immediate quarter

A downgrade from \'confident\' to \'expect\' is a sell signal even if
the headline number is unchanged. Example: HUL Q1 FY24 --- language
shifted from \'we are confident of 5-7% volume growth\' (Q4 FY23) to
\'we expect 3-5% volume growth\' (Q1 FY24). The 200 bps downward
guidance shift was the catalyst; stock de-rated -8% over 6 weeks.

**Signal 5 • Order Book Granularity**
-------------------------------------

Granular order book = de-risked. Lumpy order book = concentration +
execution risk.

-   \'47 orders across 12 customers in 5 geographies\' = high quality,
    low concentration

-   \'One large order worth Rs 800 Cr from a single customer\' =
    concentration + execution risk; the customer may cancel or push out
    delivery

-   Best practice: ask in Q&A \'what is the customer concentration of
    the order book?\' If management deflects, watch out

Example: Azad Engineering Q3 FY26 --- explicitly disclosed top 5
customers as 65% of order book (down from 78% YoY). The DECLINING
concentration was the variant signal; stock re-rated.

**Signal 6 • Margin Guidance Range vs Point Estimate**
------------------------------------------------------

A widening guidance range signals declining visibility. Compare Q4 FY26
guidance to Q3 FY26 guidance.

-   \'EBITDA margin 18-19%\' (Q3) tightening to \'17-19%\' (Q4) = lower
    visibility; concerning

-   \'EBITDA margin 18-19%\' (Q3) tightening to \'18.5-19%\' (Q4) =
    increased visibility; positive

-   Point estimate (\'we expect margin at 19%\') = highest conviction

Example: Cyient FY25 --- margin guidance widened from \'15-17%\' to
\'13-17%\' on macro slowdown commentary. Stock corrected -45% as
analysts modeled the lower end of the range.

**Signal 7 • Capex Commentary**
-------------------------------

Capex on-track with specific dates = credible. Recurring delays =
thesis-break.

-   \'Phase-2 capex on schedule, commissioning Q1 FY27\' = on track,
    hold/buy

-   \'Phase-2 capex delayed by one quarter to Q2 FY27\' first time =
    monitor for explanation

-   \'Phase-2 capex now expected Q4 FY27\' (further pushback) = sell
    signal; project has structural issues

-   Ask in Q&A: \'what is the gap between actual capex and budgeted
    capex?\' If 15%+, watch out

**Signal 8 • Customer Concentration Disclosure**
------------------------------------------------

Track top-5 customer revenue share QoQ. Decline = de-risking; sudden
increase = re-concentration risk.

-   Top-5 share moving from 78% to 65% over 4 quarters = de-risking;
    positive multiple impact

-   Top-5 share moving from 65% to 80% over 4 quarters =
    re-concentrating; negative multiple impact

-   If management refuses to disclose customer concentration, that
    itself is a signal --- they are hiding it

**Signal 9 • Receivables and Working Capital**
----------------------------------------------

DSO (Days Sales Outstanding) expanding faster than revenue growth for 2
consecutive quarters = earnings quality degrading. Ask: \'is the topline
being bought?\'

-   Revenue up 25% and receivables up 25% = healthy; in line

-   Revenue up 25% and receivables up 60% = receivables-led revenue;
    quality concern

-   Inventory days expanding 30%+ vs revenue 15% = channel stuffing or
    demand miss

-   In concall Q&A: \'what is the working capital cycle vs the prior
    4-quarter average?\'

**Signal 10 • Analyst Q&A Behavior**
------------------------------------

The Q&A reveals management confidence more than the prepared remarks.

-   Short, defensive answers (\'we don\'t comment on\...\', \'next
    question please\') = management hiding information

-   Long, granular answers with specifics = confidence; willing to
    expose details

-   Repeated dodge on a specific question (e.g., margin trajectory) =
    the answer is bad

-   Promoter / MD personally answering specific questions = strategic
    importance

-   CFO refusing follow-up clarification = number is fragile

**Signal 11 • Forward Visibility Time Horizon**
-----------------------------------------------

Whatever management discloses as the visibility horizon IS the
conviction signal.

-   \'Visibility for next 2 quarters\' = limited; book-to-bill ratio
    modest

-   \'Visibility for FY27\' (12-18 months) = strong; order book covers

-   \'Multi-year visibility through FY29\' = very strong; multi-year
    orders booked

-   Compare to prior quarters --- extending horizon = positive;
    shortening horizon = negative

**Signal 12 • Promoter Participation**
--------------------------------------

Promoter / MD personally on call (vs only CFO/IR) signals strategic
importance of the quarter.

-   Promoter usually on call + still on call this quarter = normal

-   Promoter usually on call + ABSENT this quarter without explanation =
    caution

-   Promoter rarely on call + now on call = important transition
    quarter; usually positive

-   Promoter answering opening + leaving for Q&A = ducking
    accountability; negative

**PRO TIP --- THE TRANSCRIPT IS THE TRUTH** Read the transcript, not
just listen. Search for the words \'challenging\', \'headwind\',
\'one-time\', \'exceptional\', \'normalize\', \'lumpy\', \'subject to\'.
Frequency count vs prior quarter reveals deteriorating narrative. A jump
in any of these words week-over-week is a sell signal even if the
headline numbers were a beat.

**Chapter 8 • Sector-Specific Earnings Drivers**
================================================

Every sector has its own earnings DNA. What you track for HDFC Bank
(NIM, credit cost) is completely different from what you track for Asian
Paints (volume, gross margin). The fourteen sectors below cover 90% of
Indian SMID earnings. Memorize the drivers for the sectors you own.

**BFSI Banks**
--------------

-   Net Interest Margin (NIM) --- Q/Q bps change; rising NIM = positive
    operating leverage

-   Credit cost (basis points of advances) --- should track sub-100 bps
    for retail-skewed banks; 100-150 bps for corporate-skewed

-   Return on Assets (ROA) --- target 1.4%+ for private banks; 1.0%+ for
    PSU

-   Deposit growth, especially CASA (Current + Savings Account ratio)
    --- sustainable CASA above 40% is gold; declining CASA = funding
    cost rising

-   Slippage ratio (new NPAs as % of advances) --- rising = trouble;
    declining = recovery

-   Provision Coverage Ratio (PCR) --- above 70% = comfortable; below
    60% = under-provisioned

-   Operating expense to assets --- declining = positive operating
    leverage

**IT Services**
---------------

-   Constant Currency (CC) revenue growth --- strip out FX to see
    organic growth

-   Deal TCV (Total Contract Value) --- sustained TCV growth indicates
    pipeline strength

-   Large deal pipeline (\$50m+) --- number of deals signed and in
    active pipeline

-   Attrition (LTM, last 12 months) --- below 14% = stable; above 18% =
    trouble

-   Utilization (ex-trainees) --- 80%+ healthy; below 75% means
    under-utilized capacity

-   Average Billing Rate (per FTE) --- rising = pricing power; falling =
    competitive pressure

-   BFSI vertical health (40-50% of Indian IT revenue) --- global bank
    IT spend is the leading indicator

-   Sub-contractor cost as % of revenue --- rising = capacity stress;
    falling = good utilization

**Pharma Formulations + CDMO**
------------------------------

-   US generic price erosion --- high-single-digit normal; double-digit
    = stress (Sun Pharma, Lupin US business)

-   India branded growth --- Indian Pharmaceutical Market (IPM) +9-11%
    benchmark; growth above IPM = market share gain

-   CDMO mix --- innovator pharma revenue as % of total. Higher = higher
    quality

-   US FDA inspection status --- every observation matters; consent
    decrees crush multiples (Dr Reddy\'s Bachupally 2017-18, Ranbaxy
    2008-13)

-   ANDA approvals (US FDA) --- monthly track; 5+ approvals/quarter =

-   EU filings --- diversification away from US dependence

-   Chronic vs Acute mix --- Mankind\'s 1.3x IPM thanks to chronic-heavy
    mix

**Consumer FMCG**
-----------------

-   Volume growth \> Price growth = HEALTHY. Volume growth \< Price

-   Rural vs Urban split --- rural recovery is the leading indicator for

-   Premiumization mix --- premium SKU growth as % of total; 60%+

-   Gross margin --- track quarterly; cost-input pass-through with

-   Advertising and Promotion (A&P) spend as % of revenue --- 8-12%

-   Distribution depth --- number of direct retail outlets; expanding =

**Auto OEM + Components**
-------------------------

-   Volumes --- wholesale (SIAM) + retail (FADA) monthly; divergence

-   ASP (Average Selling Price) --- rising = premiumization; falling =

-   EBITDA margin cycle --- operating leverage to volumes; raw material

-   Raw material cost pass-through --- typically 1-quarter lag; track

-   Inventory days at dealer --- 25-30 days normal; above 45 days =

-   Export mix --- export-skewed players (Bajaj Auto, TVS) less

-   EV product mix transition --- new EV launches command 80-150 bps

**Capital Goods**
-----------------

-   Order book-to-bill ratio --- above 1.2x = strong (next year\'s

-   Order inflows (quarter) --- sustained quarterly inflows = pipeline

-   Execution conversion --- order book to revenue conversion typically

-   Working capital days --- large positive number = receivables-heavy;

-   Advances from customers --- large positive = client confidence;

**Real Estate**
---------------

-   Pre-sales VALUE (Rs Cr) --- the topline of real estate

-   Pre-sales VOLUME (units) --- number of apartments sold

-   Average Realization per sq ft --- rising = premiumization or

-   Collections (Rs Cr) --- cash conversion from pre-sales

-   Volume growth (MTPA dispatched) --- should track 7-9% industry

**Power**

-   Power Purchase Agreement (PPA) mix --- long-term PPA vs merchant;

**Specialty Chemicals**

-   China+1 wins --- customers shifting from China to India for supply

**Metals**
----------

-   Like-for-like ticket size --- revenue per customer

-   Store count (new + closed) --- net additions

-   Gross margin --- declining = competitive pressure; rising =

**Chapter 9 • Valuation Frameworks**
====================================

There is no single valuation method. Different businesses require
different approaches. The institutional investor uses 3-4 methods to
triangulate and gives weight to the method best suited to the business.

**9.1 PEG (Lynch) --- PE / Growth**
-----------------------------------

Peter Lynch\'s gold standard. PE divided by earnings growth rate. Below
1.0 = cheap. 1.0-1.5 = fair. Above 2.0 = expensive.

Indian application example: Pennar Industries June 2026 --- Mcap Rs
2,950 Cr, PE 20x, EPS growth 34% = PEG 0.59 (cheap, BUY). HUL FY24 ---
PE 62x, EPS growth 10% = PEG 6.2 (very expensive, AVOID).

**9.2 EV/EBITDA Cycle Band**
----------------------------

For cyclical businesses, plot the trailing 10-year EV/EBITDA range.
Identify mean and standard deviation. Buy at mean minus 1 SD. Sell at
mean plus 1 SD.

Example: Cement sector. UltraTech 10-year EV/EBITDA range 10x-18x; mean
13x; SD 2.5x. Buy at 10.5x (mean-1SD); sell at 15.5x (mean+1SD).
Mid-cycle = mean = 13x.

**9.3 P/B for Financials**
--------------------------

Price-to-Book for banks. Combined with ROE: P/B = (ROE - g) / (CoE - g)
where g is growth and CoE is cost of equity.

HDFC Bank P/B 3.5x at ROE 17%, Cost of Equity 11%, growth 11%. Implied
P/B = (17-11)/(11-11) = undefined (terminal). At normalized growth 7%,
P/B = (17-7)/(11-7) = 2.5x --- closer to fair value.

**9.4 P/Embedded Value for Insurance**
--------------------------------------

HDFC Life 2.5-3x P/EV typical. Growth life insurers (Niva Bupa, Star)
trade at 3.5x+. Mature insurers (LIC) trade at 0.8-1.2x.

**9.5 Sum-of-Parts (SOTP)**
---------------------------

For conglomerates (Reliance, L&T, Bajaj Group, Adani Group, Tata
Sons-listed entities). Value each business separately, apply holdco
discount 15-25%.

Reliance --- RIL Energy + Reliance Retail + Jio + new businesses. Each
business valued at sector multiples then summed minus 15% holdco
discount.

**9.6 DCF (Discounted Cash Flow)**
----------------------------------

10-year explicit forecast + terminal value. Sensitive to discount rate
and terminal growth. Use as triangulation, not primary method, for
cyclicals.

**9.7 Reality Check Math**
--------------------------

Total return = (1 + EPS growth) x (1 + PE re-rating) + dividend yield -
1. If EPS grows 20% and PE re-rates from 25x to 35x, the math gives 68%
return. Sanity check whether BOTH legs are realistic. If the EPS growth
is plausible but PE re-rate requires 100x peak multiple, the re-rate leg
is unrealistic --- the actual return will be much lower.

**Chapter 10 • Ten Specific Re-Rating Triggers**
================================================

Multiple expansion is not random. Specific events trigger re-rating.
Watch for these ten events in your watchlist names.

-   1\. CAPACITY UTILIZATION CROSSING 80% --- operating leverage
    breakthrough. The next 10% utilization adds disproportionate EBITDA.
    Example: Polycab from 76% to 85% utilization drove margin expansion
    200 bps

-   2\. TOP CUSTOMER CONCENTRATION FALLING BELOW 25% --- de-risking.
    Multiple expands. Example: Azad Engineering top-5 from 78% to 65%
    drove re-rate

-   3\. NEW GEOGRAPHY/CUSTOMER/PRODUCT DISCLOSED WITH QUANTIFIED REVENUE
    --- \'Rs 50m EU contract from FY27\'. Specific number anchors
    valuation

-   4\. MARGIN EXPANSION +200 bps YoY FOR 4 CONSECUTIVE QUARTERS ---
    structural re-rating, not cyclical pop. Example: Apar Industries
    4-quarter margin expansion drove the multiple re-rate

-   5\. PROMOTER HOLDING RISING / PLEDGE FALLING TO ZERO --- governance
    re-rating. Markets reward integrity signals

-   6\. FII/DII CROSSING THRESHOLDS --- FII above 15% in SMID often
    triggers passive flows. AMFI MF holding crossing 10% same effect

-   7\. FIRST SELL-SIDE COVERAGE INITIATION --- going from 0 to 5
    brokers covered. Information asymmetry collapses. Multiple expands
    as buy-side names enter

-   8\. INDEX INCLUSION --- Nifty 500, MSCI India Standard/Small-cap,
    FTSE inclusion drives 1.5-3% passive demand. Example: Page
    Industries\' MSCI India Standard inclusion 2014 drove 25% re-rate
    over 6 months

-   9\. F&O INCLUSION --- NSE F&O eligibility drives liquidity premium
    and hedging-led institutional buying. Plus the implicit signal that
    the stock has crossed a stability threshold

-   10\. FIRST DIVIDEND / FIRST BUYBACK --- capital return discipline
    signals maturity; quality investors re-rate. Example: Mankind Pharma
    first dividend post-IPO drove re-rating

**Chapter 11 • The Three Playbooks --- Pre-Earnings, Day-Of, Post-Earnings**
============================================================================

**Playbook 1 • Pre-Earnings (T-30 to T-1 days)**
------------------------------------------------

Action items in the 30 days before a quarterly result.

-   T-30: Pull up the company\'s last 4 quarterly transcripts. Tag
    verbatim language for tone, guidance, capex, customer concentration

-   T-25: Channel checks --- call 5-10 dealers/distributors. Document
    responses

-   T-20: Peer concall read-across --- review the same-sector companies
    that reported earlier (UltraTech for Shree; HDFC Bank for ICICI).
    Identify deltas

-   T-15: Industry data review --- SIAM/FADA, AIOCD, GST collections,
    CMS, UPI

-   T-10: Sell-side estimate revision check --- direction of consensus
    EPS in last 30/60/90 days

-   T-7: Block deal + insider activity scan. Promoter selling = red
    flag; FII accumulation = positive

-   T-5: Build private estimate of revenue, EBITDA margin, PAT. Compare
    to consensus

-   T-3: Define decision rules. \'If revenue beats by 5%+ AND EBITDA
    margin expands 100+ bps AND OB grows 25%+, BUY at current price up
    to Rs X\'

-   T-1: Set order. Pre-commit either entry or exit. Eliminate day-of
    FOMO decisions

**Playbook 2 • Day-Of (Result Day)**
------------------------------------

Action items on the result day itself.

-   Pre-open: Re-check pre-committed entry / exit rules. Do not deviate

-   Result published: First read the EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (1-2 pages) for
    headline numbers

-   Open the income statement: revenue YoY, EBITDA margin Q/Q and YoY,
    PAT YoY, EPS YoY

-   Open the balance sheet: receivables vs revenue (DSO check),
    inventory, debt, cash

-   Open the cash flow statement: OCF vs PAT (quality check)

-   Open the segmental disclosure: which segment delivered the growth,
    which lagged

-   Wait for concall (typically 4-6 PM): read transcript next morning

-   Stock reaction: small beat + small move = HOLD; big beat + small
    move = BUY (market hasn\'t priced); small beat + big move = TRIM
    (over-reaction); big miss + big drop = STAY ON SIDELINE for 1-2
    weeks

**Playbook 3 • Post-Earnings (T+1 to T+30 days)**
-------------------------------------------------

Action items in the days after the print.

-   T+1: Read concall transcript fully. Tag the 12 concall signals

-   T+2: Update DCF / earnings model with new numbers

-   T+3 to T+5: Sell-side notes published --- read all 4-6 brokerage
    notes to triangulate

-   T+5: Make ADD or TRIM decision. If chart confirms (Stage 2 breakout,
    volume confirmation), ADD on closing prices

-   T+10: Re-check whisper / channel for the next quarter. The post-Q
    period reveals the new baseline

-   T+30: Decide hold horizon (3 months, 12 months, 36 months).
    Re-anchor the thesis

-   T+60: Begin the next pre-Q cycle for the following quarter

**Three position-management rules to remember**
-----------------------------------------------

-   NEVER buy a new position on the result day itself. Wait T+1 to T+5
    to absorb the data. Pre-committed positions are different --- those
    are mechanical entries

-   NEVER hold a position past 2 consecutive disappointing quarters. The
    third quarter will not save you

-   ALWAYS trim a position by 33% at +50% gain and another 33% at +120%
    gain. Let the remaining 33% run on a trailing stop. The compounding
    lives in the runner

**Chapter 12 • Real-Time Decision Scenarios**
=============================================

Six scenarios you will face this earnings season. Use the decision trees
below.

**Scenario 1 • The Headline Beat With Strong Topline**
------------------------------------------------------

Q4 FY26 print: revenue +35% YoY, EBITDA margin +200 bps, PAT +60%. Order
book +40%. Stock gaps up 8% next day.

**What to do**

-   CHECK: Is PE at premium or discount to 5-year median?

-   IF DISCOUNT: ADD 25-50% to existing position on T+1 close above
    pivot

-   IF PREMIUM: HOLD existing; do not chase. Wait T+5 for pullback

-   IF VERY EXPENSIVE (PE \> 2x growth rate): TRIM 33%. The market is
    rewarding what you can no longer add to

**Scenario 2 • The Revenue Beat But Margin Compression**
--------------------------------------------------------

Revenue +30% YoY (BEAT), but EBITDA margin -150 bps YoY (raw material
cost shock). PAT only +10% (in line). Stock falls 5%.

**What to do**

-   CHECK: Is margin compression industry-wide or company-specific?

-   IF INDUSTRY-WIDE (commodity cost up): HOLD; cyclical lag will
    reverse

-   IF COMPANY-SPECIFIC: TRIM 33%; competitive pressure breaking

-   IF MGMT EXPLAINS PASS-THROUGH IN NEXT QTR: HOLD; wait 2 quarters

**Scenario 3 • The Guidance Moderation**
----------------------------------------

Q4 print is fine (+20% PAT) but management guides next year +25% growth
(vs prior +35% guidance). Stock falls 7% Day-1, -12% in 1 week.

**What to do**

-   This is the BAJAJ FINANCE Q3 FY24 pattern. The guidance moderation
    is the killer

-   If you held the position pre-Q: TRIM 50%. Re-assess after 1 quarter
    to see if the guidance proves accurate or even moderate further

-   If you did NOT hold: DO NOT BUY. The multiple will compress for at
    least 2 quarters as analysts catch up to the new growth rate

-   Set a re-entry trigger for 30% lower price. Wait for it

**Scenario 4 • The Two-Quarter Disappointment**
-----------------------------------------------

Q3 FY26 was a small miss. Q4 FY26 is a bigger miss. Management explains
\'temporary destocking + weather\'. Stock falls -25% over 6 weeks.

**What to do**

-   Kacholia rule: two consecutive misses = TREND. Exit fully

-   The \'temporary\' explanation is almost never temporary. Wait 4
    quarters and reassess

-   Capital preservation: take the loss and redeploy to a stronger setup

**Scenario 5 • The Beat + No Move (Quality Compounder)**
--------------------------------------------------------

Asian Paints / HUL / Nestle pattern. Quarter +12% PAT, in-line
consensus. Stock flat on the day, -3% over the month.

**What to do**

-   This is the multiple-compression compounder trap. Stock not moving
    despite beats = multiple is compressing

-   Compute PEG: if PE/growth above 2.5x, TRIM gradually over 4 quarters

-   Redeploy capital to stocks where PEG is below 1.5x

**Scenario 6 • The Turnaround Quarter**
---------------------------------------

Suzlon-style --- Q4 FY25 PAT +365% vs Q4 FY24 (turnaround inflection).
Stock +25% intraday. Up to Rs 73 from Rs 11 in 2 years.

**What to do**

-   If you saw the debt clean-up coming (mid-2023 rights issue): you
    would be holding at Rs 11

-   If you bought after the Q4 FY25 print: you bought at Rs 60+; the
    remaining upside to Rs 73 was only 22%

-   LESSON: Turnarounds reward EARLY entry at the debt-clean-up event,
    not after the turnaround quarter is visible

-   To capture turnarounds, position at the CAPITAL STRUCTURE INFLECTION
    POINT (rights issue, restructuring approved). Earnings will follow
    in 6-9 months

**Chapter 13 • Future EPS Modeling**
====================================

Future EPS is the single most important number you compute. The stock at
June 2026 trades on FY28 EPS, not FY26. If you cannot model FY27 + FY28
EPS, you cannot value the stock.

**13.1 The Top-Down Approach (Revenue First)**
----------------------------------------------

Revenue = Capacity x Utilization x Realization per unit. Compute each
component separately.

-   CAPACITY: Disclosed in concall; verify against capex schedule

-   UTILIZATION: Track from quarterly disclosures; ramp from 60% to 80%
    typical over 24 months

-   REALIZATION: Track from segmental disclosures; mix-up to premium
    adds 50-200 bps

Example: KP Green Engineering --- FY26 capacity 600 acres at 60%
utilization, realization Rs 6.85 Cr/acre = Rs 2,460 Cr revenue. FY28
capacity 1,000 acres at 75% utilization, realization Rs 8 Cr/acre = Rs
6,000 Cr (2.4x). Margin held = 2.4x PAT.

**13.2 The Margin Trajectory**
------------------------------

EBITDA margin = (Gross margin) - (Employee cost / Revenue) - (Other
expenses / Revenue). Each line evolves quarterly.

-   GROSS MARGIN: Cost pass-through with 1-2 quarter lag; mix-up
    improvement 10-50 bps per year

-   EMPLOYEE COST: Wage inflation 8-10%; sub-contractor ratio shift

-   OTHER EXPENSES: Selling+freight+admin; declines with operating
    leverage as revenue grows

**13.3 Working Capital Impact**
-------------------------------

Receivables + Inventory - Payables = Working Capital. Each Rs 1 of
incremental working capital reduces FCF by Rs 1. Affects valuation via
cash flow timing, not EPS directly.

**13.4 Tax Rate Normalization**
-------------------------------

Indian corporates: typically 22% (concessional) or 25.17% (base). MAT
credit utilization can drop effective rate temporarily. Normalize to
22-25% over forecast horizon.

**13.5 Share Count and Dilution Risk**
--------------------------------------

Outstanding shares + ESOPs vesting in next 3 years + QIP risk = diluted
share count. Apply this to NPV calculations.

**13.6 EPS Target Computation**
-------------------------------

EPS = (PAT) / (Diluted Share Count). Compute FY27 + FY28 + FY29. Apply
target multiple to derive target price.

Example: Interarch Building Products. FY26 EPS Rs 78. FY28 EPS
projection: revenue +60% (capacity expansion 60%) + margin +50 bps + tax
stable = PAT Rs 270 Cr (vs Rs 165 Cr FY26) = +63%. Diluted share count
16.8m. EPS = Rs 161 (vs Rs 98 FY26). Multiple at 28x = Rs 4,508. From
current Rs 2,180, that is 107% upside to FY28 target.

**Chapter 14 • Common Traps**
=============================

These traps catch experienced investors. Memorize them.

-   TRAP 1: SINGLE-QUARTER BEAT IN CYCLICAL = TRAP. Cyclical EPS
    volatility is high. One beat could be commodity price. Three
    consecutive beats are the signal

-   TRAP 2: HIGH-PE COMPOUNDER MISS = 30% DRAW DOWN. Page Industries Q2
    FY23 fell 25% in two sessions. Position-size accordingly

-   TRAP 3: PROMOTER SELL AFTER BEAT = EXIT SIGNAL. The promoter has
    information you do not

-   TRAP 4: BLOCK DEAL AROUND RESULTS = INSTITUTIONAL INFO EDGE. Block
    deals in the 5 days before earnings show smart money knows something

-   TRAP 5: SELL-ON-NEWS PATTERN POST 6-Q RALLY. After 6 consecutive
    beats with stock 2-3x, the next print often produces a \'sell the
    news\' pattern

-   TRAP 6: ACCOUNTING RED FLAGS. OCF / PAT ratio below 60% for 3
    consecutive years = receivables-bloated

-   TRAP 7: CHANNEL CHECK CONFIRMATION BIAS. Talking only to friendly
    dealers introduces bias. Talk to 30+ across geographies

-   TRAP 8: FORWARD EARNINGS HOPE. Buying based on \'they should
    accelerate next year\' without evidence is speculation

-   TRAP 9: OVERWEIGHT YOUR FAVORITE INDUSTRY. The single biggest
    portfolio risk is sector concentration; build23 master synthesis
    warned this

-   TRAP 10: HOLDING PAST THE THESIS BREAK. Two consecutive misses +
    guidance moderation + chart breakdown = exit. Do not \'wait one more
    quarter\'

**Chapter 15 • Execution Psychology**
=====================================

The biggest gap between strategy and outcome is execution. Five
psychological forces destroy returns.

**FOMO Buying At Peak Euphoria**
--------------------------------

After a 3-bagger move, the urge to add is strongest precisely when the
multiple is most extended. Use the +50% T1 trim rule mechanically. Do
not chase post-rally.

**Anchoring To Entry Price**
----------------------------

\'I bought at Rs 800; I won\'t sell until it gets back to Rs 800.\' This
is anchoring. The current price reflects the current fundamentals. If
thesis broke, sell at current price regardless of entry.

**Loss Aversion = Holding Losers**
----------------------------------

Behavioural research: investors hold losers 2x longer than winners. The
pain of acknowledging the loss vs the marginal upside makes for
asymmetric decisions. Use mechanical stop losses to override emotion.

**Confirmation Bias On Beats**
------------------------------

After a beat, you\'ll find 10 reasons to confirm your hold thesis.
Discipline forces you to look for the 3 reasons to sell. Asymmetric
questioning protects against confirmation.

**Feel Versus The Data**
------------------------

O\'Neil: \'Markets don\'t reward feelings. They reward decisions backed
by evidence.\' Your \'feel\' that a stock is cheap or expensive is
biased. Use the data --- PEG, EV/EBITDA, growth trajectory, concall
language.

**The 30-Day Cooling Off Rule**
-------------------------------

Guy Spier: never buy a new position on result day. Wait 30 days to
absorb the data, read all sell-side notes, channel check the next
quarter, then act. The discipline of WAITING saves 80% of bad decisions.

**Chapter 17 • Twelve-Month Earnings-Driven Portfolio Strategy**
================================================================

Indian earnings season operates on the April-March fiscal year. Below is
the 12-month rhythm.

**Q1 FY (July-August): The Setup Quarter**
------------------------------------------

Q1 results published July-August. First quarter of the new fiscal year
--- sets the trajectory. Companies that beat Q1 are setting up for a
strong year. Track sectoral leaders first (Maruti, HDFC Bank,
UltraTech). Read their concalls for sector tone.

-   Pre-Q1: Update DCF models with audited FY-1 numbers

-   Q1 print: 50-name watchlist priority. Trim laggards, add winners

-   Position building: 35-40% deployed by end-September

**Q2 FY (October-November): The Swing Quarter**
-----------------------------------------------

Q2 results October-November. Half-year point. Best beats are followed by
Stage 2 acceleration. The pre-Q2 period (August-October) is when channel
checks pay off --- early read on festive consumption (Diwali) sets up Q2
winners.

-   Pre-Q2: Channel check festive demand (auto, consumer durables,
    jewellery)
