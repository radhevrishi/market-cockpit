Key Risk Management Rules
-------------------------

### Rule 1: Never Size Above Your Conviction Level

Position size should reflect the stage of the qualification cycle.
Pre-qualification = max 1%. Volume production confirmed = max 10%. No
exceptions based on narrative excitement.

### Rule 2: The Dilution Hard Stop

If any company launches an ATM offering greater than 20% of current
market cap --- evaluate whether to exit within 2 weeks. The dilution
math almost always overwhelms even a strong fundamental thesis.

### Rule 3: Thesis-Based Exits Only

Never exit based on price alone. Only exit when the THESIS breaks: a new
qualified competitor appears, a key customer cancels, or a technology
pivot eliminates the moat. A stock dropping 30% is NOT a thesis break.

### Rule 4: Conference Pre-Positioning Discipline

Build or add to positions 4--6 weeks before major industry conferences.
The window AFTER the keynote is typically too late for maximum returns.

### Rule 5: The 5-Source Validation Rule

Never commit more than 0.5% of portfolio to any position without at
least 5 independent primary source confirmations of the customer
relationships and bottleneck position. Narrative is cheap. Documentation
is rare and valuable.
